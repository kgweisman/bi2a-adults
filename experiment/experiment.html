STANFORD UNIVERSITY RESEARCH INFORMATION SHEET

PROTOCOL TITLE: Foundations of Social Reasoning
PROTOCOL DIRECTOR: Kara Weisman, graduate student in psychology
FACULTY SPONSORS: Ellen Markman, Ph.D.; Carol Dweck, Ph.D.
PROTOCOL APPROVAL DATE: December 20, 2013
PROTOCOL EXPIRATION DATE: December 20, 2016

DESCRIPTION:
You are invited to participate in a research study on the basic foundations of social reasoning. We are interested in how children and adults reason about the social and physical world, and how basic concepts of sentience and animacy might relate to more complex kinds of social cognition (such as social interaction or moral evaluation). During this study, you may be exposed to sentences about the properties or capacities of unfamiliar objects (e.g., "This one is blue"; "This one can sing"), and/or or unfamiliar objects (or pictures or short video clips featuring unfamiliar objects), such as an unusual artifact. You may be asked to respond to questions about the stories or objects (e.g., "Do you think this one can jump up and down?"; "Please describe this object").

TIME INVOLVEMENT: Your participation in this experiment will take approximately 6-8 minutes.
